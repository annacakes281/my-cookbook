Before We Begin

  • Tie hair back and out of your face
  • Clean the work surface you will be using
  • Wash hands

Eqiupment For Pizza Dough

  • Medium mixing bowl
  • Measuring cup (1-cup size) or kitchen scales
  • Measuring spoons (1/4 teaspoon and 1 tablespoon)
  • Sieve
  • Measuring jug
  • Mixing spoon - I used a metal serving spoon
  • Clingfilm
  • Teatowel

Ingredients for Pizza Dough

  • 3 cups (approx 455g) of bread flour - I used strong white bread flour, which is suitable for pizza dough - and some extra for dusting
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 7g of fast action dried yeast - I used a sachet of this
  • 1/4 teaspoon of sea or rock salt
  • 300ml of warm water - the water must be warm in order to activate the yeast

Steps for Pizza Dough

  1. Prepare all ingredients for the pizza dough
  2. Place the flour, salt, olive oil and yeast into the bowl and mix together, and then start putting the water and mixing, until the mixture is all incorpated and mixed together to a doughy consistency
  3. Dust your work surface with some flour before putting the dough onto the work surface
  4. After placing the dough on the work surface, put some flour on your hands to prevent sticking, knead the dough for about 3-5 minutes, until it has become smooth and no longer sticks to the work surface or your hands, this process is to work the gluten strands within the dough - please note that you may need to add some more flour to the work surface during this process
  5. Once the dough has reached the consistency where you can stetch it and it is smooth, it is ready to proof/prove (proofing/proving is where the dough that contains yeast is left to rest and rise), to do this, sprinkle some flour back into your mixing bowl and place the dough back into it. Cover the dough with some clingfilm, place a teatowel over it and leave it somewhere warm for around 1 hour to 1.5 hours to proof/prove
  6. While the dough is proofing, if you are making your own pizza sauce, make it now as it will need time to cool before using it on the pizza

Equipment for Pizza Toppings

  • Rolling pin
  • Flour for dusting
  • Knife to cut dough (and prep your pizza toppings if needed)
  • Baking paper/greaseproof paper/baking parchment

Ingredients for Pizza Toppings

  • Pizza sauce - either store bought around 300g, or the pizza sauce you made earlier, make sure it has cooled down before use
  • 500g of grated cheese of choice - I however used around 700g of mixed cheese as I was making several smaller pizzas, but 500g is enough for a large one, but it is up to you how much cheese to use
  • Pizza toppings of choice - I used various vegetarian toppings, mixed baby peppers and sliced shallots

Steps for Pizza Toppings

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (fan oven) / Gas mark 4
  2. Once the pizza dough has rested for between 1 hour to 1.5 hours, bring it back to your work surface, it will have doubled in size while resting in a warm place. Dust your work surface with some flour, and place the dough down on it
  3. At this point, you can deicde how to roll out your pizza, whether you want to have smaller ones, like I did or have a larger one (like one of the images at the top of the page). Use the knife to section into smaller bits of dough, then roll into a ball-like shape and start to roll it out, remeber to roll in both directions and then also flipping it over to the other side to get a sort of circular shape (although mine didnt't really end up like this, I was still happy with what I got as it's still going to be pizza). You can make different shapes, that is your preference, once rolled out, lightly sprinle some flour onto your baking paper and place the rolled out dough onto it
  4. Repeat step 3 until you have rolled out all the dough to your liking

    5. Now you are ready to start spreading your pizza sauce onto the base and placing your toppings on


  5. Put some pizza sauce onto the base and spread it out across the whole base to your liking, I enjoy quiet a bit of sauce on mine, I also added some sundried tomato pesto to my base with the pizza sauce as this is a personal preference
  6. After putting the sauce onto the pizza base, you're ready to start spreading your cheese and adding your toppings, do this to your liking. After putting my toppings on the pizza, I put a bit of cheese on top of the pizza and added some of my left over home made pizza sauce on top of the pizza
  7. Place the pizza onto a baking tray if you are cooking it now

    Pizza Cooking Times

    Now you are ready to cook your pizza, please note that cooking times may vary - the cooking times listed below are for an fan oven on 180°C


  • Large pizza (similar to image at top of page): 18 minutes
  • Small-medium pizza: 12-15 minutes
  • Once cooked allow to rest for a minute, then slice however you like and enjoy your home-made pizza! 😁
