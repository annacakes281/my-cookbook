Before We Begin

  • Tie hair back and out of your face
  • Clean the work surface you will be using
  • Wash hands

Eqiupment For Pizza Dough

  • Medium mixing bowl
  • Measuring cup (1-cup size) or kitchen scales
  • Measuring spoons (1/4 teaspoon and 1 tablespoon)
  • Sieve
  • Measuring jug
  • Mixing spoon - I used a metal serving spoon
  • Clingfilm
  • Teatowel

Ingredients for Pizza Dough

  • 3 cups (approx 455g) of bread flour - I used strong white bread flour, which is suitable for pizza dough - and some extra for dusting
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 7g of fast action dried yeast - I used a sachet of this
  • 1/4 teaspoon of sea or rock salt
  • 300ml of warm water - the water must be warm in order to activate the yeast

Steps for Pizza Dough

  1. Prepare all ingredients for the pizza dough
  2. Place the flour, salt, olive oil and yeast into the bowl and mix together, and then start putting the water and mixing, until the mixture is all incorpated and mixed together to a doughy consistency
  3. Dust your work surface with some flour before putting the dough onto the work surface
  4. After placing the dough on the work surface, put some flour on your hands to prevent sticking, knead the dough for about 3-5 minutes, until it has become smooth and no longer sticks to the work surface or your hands, this process is to work the gluten strands within the dough - please note that you may need to add some more flour to the work surface during this process
  5. Once the dough has reached the above consistency, it is ready to proof/prove (proofing/proving is where the dough that contains yeast is left to rest and rise), to do this, sprinkle some flour back into your mixing bowl and place the dough back into it. Cover the dough with some clingfilm, place a teatowel over it and leave it somewhere warm for around 1 hour to 1.5 hours to proof/prove
  6. While the dough is proofing, if you are making your own pizza sauce, make it now as it will need time to cool before using it on the pizza
